Micro-Bio-Battery

Design

Generation of the pH-value with the transfer of calcium ions using ionophore

Two different droplets are prepared in W/O emulsion. One of which are made by inserting great amounts of fluorescent ion beads charged with calcium ion inside the droplet. We will call this droplet #1. The other droplet is created by inserting calcium indicator instead. And call this droplet #2. After both droplets are prepared, connect them together (The junction of the two droplets is a bilayer). The ionomycin in the emulsion catches the calcium ion inside droplet #1 by the droplet interface. The ionomycin will then move through the junction to droplet #2 and there it unleashes the calcium ion. This causes the electrical neutrality inside droplet #2 to deviate. In order to resolve this electric bias, the protons (generated from the ionization of water inside droplet #2) bond to the same ionomycin and they get transmitted to droplet #1. By this transition, the concentration of protons inside droplet #1 increases. On the other hand, because calcium ions flow in, droplet #2 becomes basic. Thus, the proton concentration decreases. As a result, the pH-value of droplet #1 decreases and that of droplet #2 increases, thus generating pH difference between the two.
