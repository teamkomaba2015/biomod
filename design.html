Micro-Bio-Battery

Design

Generation of the pH-value with the transfer of calcium ions using ionophore

Two different types of droplets are prepared in W/O emulsion. One of which is made by inserting great amounts of fluorescent ion beads charged with calcium ion inside the droplets. (=droplet #1) The other droplet is created by inserting calcium indicator instead. (=droplet #2) After preparation, both droplets are brought near and joined. Near the interface of the droplet #1, the ionomycin in the emulsion exchanges its hydrogen ion with the calcium ion inside droplet #1. Therefore ionomycin releases its hydrogen ion into the droplet #1 and captures calcium ions. Since the ion concentration of the droplet #1 is much higher than that of the droplet #2, by the law of entropy increase, the calcium bonded with the ionomycin permeates through the droplet #1. Ionomycin’s membrane permeability disposition allows itself to ooze out to the junction surrounded by the hydrophobic part of droplet #1 and #2. Then ionomycin with calcium ions permeates into the droplet #2.


There ionomycin releases calcium ion and exchanges it with the hydrogen ion (proton) generated by the ionization of water.


The ionomycin that releases the calcium ion floats into the emulsion as it is. The manipulation above is repeated until the concentration of the calcium ion of both droplet #1 and #2 is equal. As a whole, the droplet #1 becomes acidic because hydrogen ion flows in and droplet #2 becomes basic because hydrogen ion flows out. As a result, the pH-value of the droplet #1 decreases and that of the droplet #2 increases, thus generating pH difference between the two.
