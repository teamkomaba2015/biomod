Micro-Bio-Battery

Results and Discussion

In the experiment, our purpose is to confirm the movement of calcium ion between the two kinds of droplet: one droplets that contain calcium indicator (fluo3) and EDTA (in C1 solution), and the others that contain fluorescent beads charged with calcium ion (in C2 solution). We observed two kinds of solution, D1 and D2. D1 solution is the mixture of C1 solution, C2 solution and ionomycin solution.D2 solution is the mixture of C1 solution and ionomycin solution. The fluorescent beads in C1 shines in the red excitation light. Combined with calcium ion, the calcium indicator shines in the green excitation light.

Figure 1.
Microscope photograph of droplets in D1 of 100-fold dilution.

A is the picture taken in the bright field.
B is in the in the green excitation light.
C is in the red excitation light.



D1: In the picture-A, which is taken in the bright field, many globular droplets are observed. Especially, we want to take attention to the droplets in the red circle. (Droplet-a1, Droplet-a2, Droplet-b) Two types of droplets can be seen. One is the droplet of which inside is white (Droplet-a1, Droplet-a2). We call this type of droplet the white type droplet. The other one is the droplet of which inside is black (Droplet-b). We call this type of droplet the black type droplet. In the latter type droplets, Droplet-b, tiny particles are seen moving (Brownian motion). So it can be suggested that the black type droplet is the one that contain so many fluorescent beads. It can also be suggested that the white type droplet is the one that contain calcium indicator. In the picture-B, which is taken in the green excitation light, Droplet-a1 and Droplet-a2 shines so brightly. On the other hand, in the picture-C, which is taken in the red excitation light, only Droplet-b shines, and Droplet-a1 or Droplet-a2 does not shine. So it is sure that only Droplet-b (the black one) contain fluorescent beads, and that Droplet-a1 or Droplet-a2 (white type droplets) certainly contain calcium ion.

Figure 2. Microscope photograph of droplets in D3 of 100-fold dilution.
D is the picture taken in the bright field.
E is in the in the green excitation light.


D3: In the picture-D, which is taken in the bright field, many globular droplets are observed. These droplets are the white type droplets, which are observed in the picture A. In the picture-F, which is taken in the green excitation light, droplets scarcely shine.
So it can be confirmed that in the droplets from C1 solution do not contain calcium ion.
(=The white type droplets, where the calcium indicator is, do not contain calcium ion.)

From these data, we can make sure the movement of calcium ion from the black type droplets to the white type droplets by ionomycin.
