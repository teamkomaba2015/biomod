Nano Bio Battery

Abstract

Recently, research on bio-batteries, which can generate electricity from energy produced by biological activities, is becoming popular partly due to development of artificial organs and body-machine interfaces. In this project we propose a new kind of battery made of tiny water droplets enclosed by lipid layer in oil phase, which can produce electric energy inside human body. Since batteries in general work by oxidation-reduction reactions in electric potential difference, our first goal in this project is to produce electric potential difference in a pair of water droplets.
In oil phase, two water droplets are joined with alpha-hemolysin. Calcium ions (cations) and anionic fluorescent beads are put inside one of the two droplets, while calcium indicators inside the other with no ions. Although both the two kinds of ions (cations and anions) try to move to the other side through the hole made by alpha-hemolysin in the junction of the two spheres, the smaller ones (=cations: Ca2+) can only pass through the hole selectively due to the difference of the size of the ions against the hole. Consequently, electric potential difference is produced.
By connecting the two droplets by a nanoscale electrode with Mg at its ends, Mg2+ ions melt into the negatively charged droplet due to the difference in ionization tendency to make up a shortage of cations. In the other droplet, hydrogen ions receive electrons, and produce hydrogen molecules. In this process, electrons flow along the electric potential difference. This is the mechanism of our Nano-Bio-Battery. By putting all this w/o emulsion system into water, a stabilized w/o/w emulsion complex is obtained.
In the future, Nano-Bio-Battery will be implanted into human body, and produce electricity safely and stably, resulting in practical use in medical field.
